# Crab Labs Homepage: B2B Sales & Marketing Expert Review

**Reviewer:** VP of Marketing perspective (18 years B2B engineering services experience)
**Date:** February 2026
**Page Analyzed:** `/src/pages/index.astro`

---

## Executive Summary

**Conversion Impact Score: 6.5/10**

The Crab Labs homepage has solid fundamentals but leaves significant conversion potential on the table. The messaging is clear and the value proposition is articulated, but the page lacks the depth of social proof, urgency mechanisms, and objection-handling that sophisticated B2B buyers expect from a considered purchase.

### What's Working
- Clean, professional design that signals competence
- Transparent pricing (rare and valuable in this industry)
- Clear differentiation ("no middlemen," fixed pricing, IP ownership)
- Appropriate technical tone for the audience
- Strong hero stats that quantify the offering

### What's Hurting Conversion
- Single testimonial from one company (insufficient social proof)
- No case study outcomes or metrics visible on homepage
- Missing urgency/scarcity signals
- No risk reversal language (guarantees, pilot options)
- Weak objection handling for enterprise/procurement concerns
- Hero CTAs are generic ("Book a call," "Get a quote")
- No named engineers or faces visible

## Pass 1: First Impression and Value Proposition Clarity

### Above the Fold Analysis (0-5 seconds)

**What I See:**
1. "Systems Online" indicator - unclear meaning, wastes prime real estate
2. "Medical & Industrial Electronics." - good category statement
3. "Hardware, firmware, and compliance testing. Senior engineers, fixed pricing, no middlemen."
4. Two CTAs: "Book a call" and "Get a quote"
5. Four stats: 5 engineers, 30+ years, $150/hr, 100% IP

**Value Proposition Clarity: 7/10**

The visitor understands WHAT Crab Labs does within 5 seconds. However, the WHY CHOOSE CRAB LABS is buried in the subhead and stats. The value prop is competent but not compelling.

**Issues:**
- "Systems Online" is insider jargon that means nothing to prospects
- The hero title focuses on WHAT (electronics) not the OUTCOME (product success, faster time to market, certification passed)
- "No middlemen" is a negative frame - tells me what you're NOT, not what you ARE

**Recommendation:**
The hero should lead with the outcome, not the category. Engineering buyers don't wake up thinking "I need medical electronics." They think "I need to hit my FDA submission deadline" or "My last firm missed the deadline and I can't let that happen again."

**Header:** "What we do"

This is generic. Every services company says "What we do."

This is excellent. The price range anchors expectations and the IP ownership statement is strong. This should be more prominent.

This subhead is strong - it signals expertise and implies a pain point (shortcuts = failures = past bad experiences).

- No project count ("50+ medical device projects")

### Lab Section

**Header:** "Test before you ship"
**Subhead:** "In-house EMC pre-scans, environmental testing, and automated validation. Find problems before certification, not during."

This is strong messaging. The "before certification, not during" implies a pain point (failed certification tests = expensive delays) without belaboring it.

**Equipment List:**
- Oscilloscopes (Up to 8 GHz)
- Spectrum Analyzers (Up to 6 GHz)
- Network Analyzers (Up to 6 GHz)
- EMC Test Equipment (Antennas, LISN, TEM cell)
- Thermal Chamber (Temperature & humidity)

**Issues:**
- Equipment specs are technical details that only engineers will appreciate
- Missing: What does having this equipment MEAN for the client?
- No lab photos or video - missed opportunity to show credibility

**Recommended Addition:**

```
"Our in-house lab means we iterate in days, not weeks. When a design needs changes, we assemble, test, and revise without waiting for external vendors."
```

### Pricing Section

**Header:** "Transparent rates"
**Subhead:** "Fixed-price projects when scope is clear. Hourly when it's not. Either way, no surprises."

This is good positioning. The "no surprises" language addresses a common B2B anxiety.

**Pricing Display:**
- Standard: $150/hr
- Startups & academia: $135/hr
- Non-profits: $140/hr
- Rush (+50%): $225/hr

**What's Working:**
- Public pricing is a bold differentiator (most competitors hide rates)
- Tiered pricing shows flexibility and accessibility
- Rush premium sets expectations

**What's Missing:**
- No comparison to market ("Industry typical: $175-250/hr")
- No explanation of what's included vs. excluded
- Fixed-price model is mentioned but not explained

**Recommended Addition:**

```
"Why we publish our rates: Most firms hide pricing to extract maximum budget. We'd rather you know what to expect from the start."
```

### Insights Section

Displays latest 3 blog posts.

**Issue:** Blog content is generic thought leadership. On a conversion-focused homepage, this space could be used for:
- Customer success stories
- ROI calculators
- Downloadable assets (checklists, guides)

### Testimonial Section

**Current State:** Single testimonial from Myles Ainley, CEO of Atmos Research & Development.

**Quote:** "Crab Labs played a key role in refining and validating our product development. We couldn't have done it without them."

**Critical Issues:**
1. Only ONE testimonial - suggests limited client base or inability to collect social proof
2. Quote is generic - could be said about any firm
3. No outcome metrics - what was refined? What was validated?
4. Unknown company - "Atmos Research & Development" has no brand recognition
5. No logo visible in this section (though logo path exists in data)

**Severity: HIGH** - This is the weakest section on the page. B2B buyers, especially for considered purchases, rely heavily on social proof.

## Pass 4: Conversion Path Analysis

### Primary CTAs

| CTA | Location | Text | Destination | Effectiveness |
|-----|----------|------|-------------|---------------|
| Hero Primary | Above fold | "Book a call" | cal.com | Medium |
| Hero Secondary | Above fold | "Get a quote" | /quote | Medium |
| Services | Mid-page | "View all services" | /services | Low - weak action |
| Industries | Mid-page | "See case studies" | /case-studies | Medium |
| Lab | Mid-page | "See our capabilities" | /capabilities | Low |
| Pricing | Mid-page | "Get a quote" | /quote | Medium |
| Insights | Mid-page | "View all" | /insights | Low |
| Nav | Persistent | "Get a Quote" | /quote | Medium |

### CTA Effectiveness Issues

1. **"Book a call" vs "Get a quote"** - What's the difference? When should I use which? This creates decision paralysis.

2. **No micro-conversions** - The only options are "talk to sales" or "request quote." Missing:
   - Download a resource (checklist, guide)
   - Subscribe to insights
   - Use a calculator/estimator
   - Watch a video

3. **CTA copy is generic** - "Book a call" could be any company. Better: "Talk to an Engineer" or "Schedule Technical Consultation"

4. **No urgency or scarcity** - No indication of:
   - Limited capacity ("We take on 8-10 projects per year" - this IS on the case studies page but not homepage)
   - Response time ("Get a quote in 24 hours")
   - Seasonal availability

### Friction Points

1. **No live chat** - Engineering buyers often have quick questions before committing to a call
2. **Phone number buried in footer** - Some buyers prefer to call; make it accessible
3. **No FAQ on homepage** - Common questions go unanswered
4. **Quote form requires commitment** - No "just exploring" option
5. **No NDA mention until quote page** - Buyers worry about sharing IP early

### Recommended CTA Improvements

```
Before: "Book a call"
After: "Talk to an Engineer (20 min)"

Before: "Get a quote"
After: "Get Fixed-Price Quote (48h Response)"

Add: "Not ready to talk? Download our PCB Review Checklist"
Add: "See how we helped [client] achieve [outcome]"
```

## Summary

The Crab Labs homepage has a solid foundation: clear services, transparent pricing, and an appropriate professional tone. However, it's leaving conversion on the table by relying on a single testimonial, missing client logos, and providing no risk-reversal language.

The biggest gap is social proof. For a considered B2B purchase in the $10k-$100k range, buyers need significantly more evidence that Crab Labs can deliver. The current page reads as "we're competent and transparent" when it should read as "we've done this successfully for dozens of clients like you."

The quick wins are low-effort, high-impact changes that can be implemented immediately. The strategic improvements require more investment but will meaningfully improve conversion for larger, more cautious buyers.

**Bottom Line:** This is a 6.5/10 homepage with clear potential to reach 8.5/10 with focused improvements in social proof, objection handling, and conversion path optimization.
